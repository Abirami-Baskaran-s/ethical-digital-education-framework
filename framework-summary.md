# Framework Summary: Ethical & Culturally-Responsive Digital Education

This framework outlines the governance, data, system, and engagement principles required to design **ethical, compliant, and data-driven digital education systems**, aligned with **Te Tiriti o Waitangi** and **NZQA standards**.

---

## 1. Governance & Principles

**Context / Resources**
- Te Tiriti o Waitangi
- Māori Data Sovereignty
- GDPR
- NZ Privacy Act 2020
- NZQA & TEC requirements

**Key Highlights**
- Ensures Treaty-aligned governance and accountability
- Protects Māori data rights and cultural identity
- Aligns analytics and systems with legal and regulatory standards
- Addresses risks in online delivery and academic quality assurance

**Operational Value**
- Clear compliance framework
- Reduced regulatory and reputational risk
- Governance-first analytics design

---

## 2. Data Management

**Context / Resources**
- Ethical AI principles
- Informed consent processes
- Equity data monitoring (Māori, Pasifika, disabilities)
- Secure cloud infrastructure

**Key Highlights**
- Transparent and explainable use of AI
- Ethical data collection and usage
- Equity-focused monitoring and reporting
- Secure, scalable data storage

**Operational Value**
- Trustworthy analytics
- Bias-aware reporting
- Strong data security and privacy controls

---

## 3. System Integration

**Context / Resources**
- Student Management System (SMS)
- Learning Management System (LMS)
- Audit trails and compliance reporting
- Cultural integrity in data (Māori names and values)

**Key Highlights**
- Centralised source of truth for student data
- Seamless academic and support system integration
- Strong auditability and traceability
- Respectful handling of culturally significant data

**Operational Value**
- Reduced manual work and data duplication
- Improved reporting accuracy
- Better risk and performance monitoring

---

## 4. Engagement & Training

**Context / Resources**
- Staff ethical data and cultural competency training
- Student data rights awareness
- Māori partnership models

**Key Highlights**
- Builds staff capability in ethical and digital practices
- Empowers students through data transparency
- Progresses towards meaningful Māori partnership

**Operational Value**
- Higher system adoption
- Increased trust and engagement
- Sustainable change management

---

- Compliance-aligned reporting and dashboards
